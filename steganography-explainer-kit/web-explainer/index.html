The big idea

Two ways to keep a secret

Most apps lock your message so nobody can read it. But a locked box still screams "there's a secret here!" Steganography does something sneakier: it hides the secret inside something boring, so nobody even looks.

🔒 Cryptography

🔒➡️🔣

Scrambles the meaning. People can see there's a secret — they just can't read it. (A locked box everyone notices.)

🦎 Steganography

🐱…🔍✨

Hides the existence. It looks like an ordinary photo or text. Nobody suspects a thing. (A secret in a disguise.)

Cryptography hides what you say.
Steganography hides that you said anything.

Two more spots

…and in voice & files

🎧 A face in the noise

Sound is samples too. Hide a message in the quietest parts — or hide a whole picture inside the sound. It plays like static, but the spectrogram (the sound's "x-ray") reveals it.

📁 The secret pocket

Every file has an official "THE END." Apps stop reading there — so you can staple secret data right after it. A single file can be a meme and secretly a zip full of files.

Wait, what

Secrets are everywhere

🖨️ Your printer is a snitch

Most color printers stamp every page with near-invisible yellow dots — encoding the printer's serial number and the date. You've been signing your printouts for decades.

🤖 AI images have invisible tattoos

AI-generated images often carry a hidden watermark baked into the pixels (like Google's SynthID) — invisible to you, survives screenshots and crops.

🐛 "It's just a picture" — not always

Hackers have hidden real malware inside ordinary images. One attack stashed stolen credit-card numbers inside a store's own product photos.

🏛️ Older than computers

2,500 years ago a Greek ruler tattooed a message on a slave's shaved head, waited for the hair to grow back, and sent him as a "blank" messenger.
