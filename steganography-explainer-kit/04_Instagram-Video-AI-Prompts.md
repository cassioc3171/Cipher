# Instagram Video — AI Generation Prompts (Seedance 2.0 structured)

Built with the Seedance 2.0 effects-breakdown format. Works as-is for Seedance; the per-shot prompts at the bottom also drop into Veo, Runway, Kling, or Pika.

> **Read this first — how to actually produce it.** AI video generators are great at *motion, environments, and effects* but unreliable at *legible on-screen text* and *a consistent cartoon mascot across shots.* So: generate the **backgrounds / effect shots** with AI using the prompts below, then **composite Steg (from `/images`) and all captions in an editor** (CapCut / After Effects / Premiere). That gives you a perfect, repeatable mascot and crisp text. Treat the AI as your VFX b-roll engine, not your typesetter.

## SECTION 1 — SHOT-BY-SHOT EFFECTS TIMELINE

Style bible for every shot: vertical 9:16, dark **midnight (#0B1020)** base, neon glow, "cyber but cuddly." Ordinary elements are muted; **hidden secrets glow Signal Green (#3DDC84)**. Cipher cobalt **#2563EB** + violet **#5A0FC8** accents. Subtle film grain + faint pixel-dispersion particles drifting in the background throughout.

```
SHOT 1 (00:00–00:03) — The Innocent Photo
• EFFECT: slow push-in (digital zoom, ~5%) + subtle pixel-shimmer undercurrent
• A warm, ordinary phone selfie of a cat fills the full vertical frame. Calm, muted grade.
• Beneath the surface, a faint green glow pulses through the pixels once, like a heartbeat — hinting something is hidden.
• Camera: locked, almost imperceptible slow push-in to build unease.
• EXIT: the image edge "peels" at one corner like a sticker lifting → leads to Shot 2.

SHOT 2 (00:03–00:08) — Steg Peels Off
• EFFECT: peel/reveal transition + pixel-dispersion bloom
• The cat photo peels away to reveal it WAS Steg the pixel-chameleon camouflaged on top of it. His skin settles from cat-colors to Cipher cobalt. Tail dissolves into drifting square bits.
• He gives a small two-finger wave and a smug grin; one eye swivels to camera.
• Camera: gentle handheld bob, friendly.
• EXIT: Steg flicks his tail → screen splits down the middle into Shot 3.

SHOT 3 (00:08–00:14) — Lock vs Disguise (split screen)
• EFFECT: vertical split-screen + glow contrast
• LEFT half: a glowing, ornate LOCKED BOX on a pedestal under a spotlight — a crowd of little eyeball icons all stare at it. RIGHT half: a boring grey rock nobody looks at; Steg is faintly camouflaged ON the rock.
• Lighting tells the story: the box is loud and over-lit; the rock side is calm and ignored.
• Camera: static, symmetrical.
• EXIT: hard whip-pan left→right (motion-blur smear) into Shot 4.

SHOT 4 (00:14–00:16) — The One-Liner
• EFFECT: text-slam impact + screen shake (composite text in post)
• Plain midnight frame. Pixel particles freeze for emphasis. (Caption added in editor: "Crypto hides what you say. Steg hides THAT you said anything.")
• Camera: tiny punch-in on the beat.
• EXIT: quick green bloom flash → Shot 5.

SHOT 5 (00:16–00:21) — TEXT: invisible letters
• EFFECT: macro slide reveal + green glow type-on
• A single chat bubble "hey what's up :)" floats center. A magnifier/lens glides left→right across it; INSIDE the gaps between letters, glowing green characters fade in (the hidden message).
• Camera: slow lateral track following the lens.
• EXIT: lens lens-flare wipe → Shot 6.

SHOT 6 (00:21–00:26) — IMAGE: change the last digit
• EFFECT: extreme digital zoom (scale-in) into pixels + value flip
• Two identical cat photos sit side by side. Camera punches HARD into one until individual pixels fill the frame as a grid of RGB number tiles; the last digit of each ticks 232→233 and glows green.
• This is a SIGNATURE VISUAL EFFECT — the "invisible change" made visible.
• Camera: aggressive scale-in, then settle.
• EXIT: pixels scatter (dispersion) and reform into Shot 7's waveform.

SHOT 7 (00:26–00:31) — VOICE: a face in the noise
• EFFECT: waveform→spectrogram morph (SIGNATURE VISUAL EFFECT)
• A calm horizontal audio waveform pulses gently. It morphs/unfolds upward into a colorful spectrogram, and within the frequencies a simple face (or the word "HELLO") is clearly drawn in glowing green.
• Camera: slow vertical tilt up as the spectrogram "grows."
• EXIT: spectrogram collapses to a single point → Shot 8.

SHOT 8 (00:31–00:34) — FILE: secret pocket
• EFFECT: 2.5D drawer slide + depth parallax
• A meme JPG sits flat. A hidden drawer slides out from behind its bottom edge, stuffed with glowing secret pages and a small zip-folder icon.
• Camera: slight dolly to reveal depth/parallax behind the image.
• EXIT: drawer snaps shut, push-in to the phone in Shot 9.

SHOT 9 (00:34–00:42) — You Already Do This
• EFFECT: dual thought-bubble composite + soft focus background
• A hand holds a phone posting a song-lyric story. Two thought bubbles rise: one (mom) shows a music note and a calm smile; the other (best friend) shows a broken heart and a worried face. Same post, two meanings.
• Camera: intimate slow push toward the phone screen.
• EXIT: heartbeat-synced dip to black → Shot 10.

SHOT 10 (00:42–00:46) — The Printer Snitch
• EFFECT: UV-sweep reveal + macro
• A white printed page glides out of a printer. A purple UV light sweeps across and reveals a grid of tiny glowing yellow dots.
• Camera: top-down macro, light sweep left→right.
• EXIT: dots lift off the page and swarm → reform into the Cipher logo for Shot 11.

SHOT 11 (00:46–00:50) — Cipher: lock it, then hide it
• EFFECT: logo assembly from pixels + lock-snap + camouflage blend
• The swarming bits assemble into the Cipher "C" (solid-to-dispersing pixel logo). A padlock snaps shut over a message, THEN Steg blends the whole thing into an ordinary chat bubble that looks empty.
• Steg gives a final wink. (Editor adds: "Cipher — lock it. then hide it. link in bio.")
• Camera: settle to a clean, centered hero shot.
• EXIT: final green sparkle; hold on end card.
```

## SECTION 2 — MASTER EFFECTS INVENTORY

1. **Pixel dispersion / reform** (used 5x) — Shots 1, 2, 6, 10, 11. The brand-signature motif: solids dissolving into drifting squares and reassembling. Ties every transition to the Cipher logo.
2. **Glow reveal (Signal Green)** (used 6x) — Shots 1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 10. The visual grammar of "secret revealed." Anything hidden glows green on appearance.
3. **Digital zoom punch** (used 3x) — Shots 1 (gentle), 6 (aggressive scale-in), 9 (intimate push). Drives intensity contrast.
4. **Morph transition** (used 2x) — Shot 6→7 (pixels→waveform), Shot 7 (waveform→spectrogram). The two signature "aha" moments.
5. **Peel / drawer reveal** (used 2x) — Shots 2 (photo peel), 8 (file drawer). Physical "there was something behind it" gags.
6. **Whip pan / motion-blur smear** (used 1x) — Shot 3→4 exit. Energy spike before the one-liner.
7. **Split screen** (used 1x) — Shot 3. Crypto vs steg, side by side.
8. **UV / light sweep** (used 1x) — Shot 10. The printer-dots reveal.
9. **Bloom flash** (used 2x) — Shot 4 entry, Shot 11 close. Punctuation.
10. **Composited text slam** (added in editor, all caption beats) — Shots 4, 5–11 labels + CTA.

## SECTION 3 — EFFECTS DENSITY MAP

```
00:00–00:03 = LOW    (slow push-in + 1 glow pulse — 2 effects in 3s) — calm, baited
00:03–00:08 = MEDIUM (peel transition + dispersion + character motion — 3 effects in 5s)
00:08–00:16 = MEDIUM (split screen + whip pan + text slam + bloom — building)
00:16–00:26 = HIGH   (lens reveal + glow type-on + extreme zoom + value flip + dispersion — signature stretch)
00:26–00:34 = HIGH   (waveform→spectrogram morph + drawer parallax + glow — the two "aha" beats)
00:34–00:42 = LOW    (one clean intimate push + soft bubbles — the emotional breath)
00:42–00:50 = HIGH   (UV sweep + swarm-to-logo + lock snap + camouflage blend + sparkle — climax/CTA)
```
Contrast logic: the emotional "you already do this" beat (34–42s) is deliberately the calmest moment, so the CTA climax hits harder.

---

## SECTION 4 — ENERGY ARC

- **Act 1 — Bait (0–8s):** Start deceptively calm and ordinary (a cat photo), then the mascot literally peels off it. Establishes the thesis — *things are not what they seem* — with charm, not noise.
- **Act 2 — Build (8–34s):** Escalating reveals. Lock-vs-disguise frames the idea, then the four hiding spots ramp density to two signature morphs (pixels→sound→image-in-sound). This is the educational core, paced as a chain of "wait, what" reveals.
- **Act 3a — The Turn (34–42s):** Energy drops on purpose. Quiet, personal: *you already do this.* The viewer goes from spectator to participant. This is the line they'll repeat.
- **Act 3b — Land (42–50s):** Re-accelerate into the printer-dots gag and the Cipher climax — lock snaps, secret vanishes into an innocent bubble, Steg winks. Energy resolves on the product as the natural payoff, not an ad bolted on.
